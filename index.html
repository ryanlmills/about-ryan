About Rye in Mills

Rye (Secale Cereale) is a common grass grown as a grain and used for foods such as flour, bread, oats, and even alcohols such as whiskey and vodka.
Rye in mills is a very common form of manufacturing the grass.

Rye in mills starts the process of crushing and grinding in order to process it into flour for consumption, rye flour has lower gluten than wheat flour for the health conscious.
Rye in mills is a very common site in the industrialized world.

Rye is grown primarily in Eastern, Central and Northern Europe.
